Privacy Policy

Effective 30.05.2026 · Last updated 30.05.2026

Foodie helps you save, organize, and cook recipes. This policy explains what information the app handles, who processes it on our behalf, and the rights you have under the EU/EEA General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Foodie is built to keep your data on your device and in your own private iCloud wherever possible. We do not sell your data, and we do not use it to build advertising profiles.

1. Who is responsible for your data

Foodie is developed and operated by Mikael Walde, a sole proprietorship (enkeltpersonforetak) registered in Norway. For the purposes of the GDPR, we are the data controller for the limited personal data described below.

You can contact us about privacy at purwalbsn@gmail.com .

2. The data Foodie handles

Recipes and content you create

Recipes you save, edit, import, or plan — along with your grocery lists and meal plans — are stored locally on your device and synced to your own private iCloud account through Apple's CloudKit. This content stays within Apple's ecosystem under your Apple ID. We cannot see, access, or retrieve it.

Recipe import using AI

When you import a recipe from a web link or video, Foodie sends the relevant content (such as the page text or the media you are importing) to Google's Gemini models via Firebase AI so it can be turned into a structured recipe. This processing is performed to deliver a feature you have asked for. Where your device supports it, some processing may instead run on-device through Apple Intelligence, in which case the content does not leave your device.

To help us monitor and improve the quality of recipe extraction, Google retains samples of the content sent for import — such as the recipe text or link you provide — together with the recipe generated in response. This diagnostic data is stored in Google Cloud Logging under Google's privacy practices and may include personal information contained in the content you import.

Usage and analytics data

If enabled, Foodie uses Firebase Analytics to understand how features are used (for example, which screens are opened and how often imports happen), together with a pseudonymous app instance identifier and basic device information. This helps us improve the app. You can turn analytics off at any time in Settings → Privacy → Share Analytics; when off, no analytics events are collected.

Crash and performance data

Foodie uses Firebase Crashlytics to collect crash reports and performance diagnostics (such as crash logs and basic device and OS information) so we can find and fix problems. This data is not used to identify you personally.

Subscriptions and purchases

Foodie Pro is sold as an in-app subscription. Payments are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store — we never see or store your payment card details. We use RevenueCat to manage and verify your subscription status, which involves a pseudonymous app user identifier and your purchase status.

Reminders and grocery lists

If you export a grocery list to Apple Reminders, Foodie writes that list to your Reminders with your permission. This happens on your device, and the data is not transmitted to us.

4. Legal bases for processing

Under the GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract — to provide the app's core features, including AI recipe import and subscription management.
  • Legitimate interests — to keep the app stable and secure through crash and performance reporting, and to understand usage in aggregate. You can object to analytics by switching it off.
  • Consent — where consent is required, for example any permission you grant (such as access to Reminders), which you can withdraw at any time in iOS Settings.

5. How long we keep data

Your recipes and content remain on your device and in your iCloud until you delete them or delete the app. Analytics and crash data are retained by our processors for limited periods in line with their standard retention settings, after which they are deleted or aggregated. Diagnostic data captured during AI recipe import is retained in Google Cloud Logging according to its configured retention period. Subscription records are retained for as long as needed to manage your subscription and meet legal and accounting obligations.

6. International transfers

Some of our processors (in particular Google and RevenueCat) may process data on servers located outside the EU/EEA, including in the United States. Where this happens, the transfer is protected by appropriate safeguards such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses.

7. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Object to or restrict certain processing
  • Request a copy of your data in a portable format
  • Withdraw any consent you have given

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at purwalbsn@gmail.com . You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Norwegian Data Protection Authority, Datatilsynet (datatilsynet.no).

8. Children

Foodie is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your country), and we do not knowingly collect personal data from them.
